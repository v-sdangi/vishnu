Leadership Message



On the path to net zero: IMCO releases its interim targets and climate action plan

I am pleased to share IMCO's interim climate targets for 2030, aimed towards achieving the 1.5°C temperature goal of the Paris Agreement and net zero emissions by 2050.

Managing climate change and supporting the global transition to a net zero emissions economy are among IMCO's top ESG priority areas. We manage both the physical and transition risks associated with climate change and seek opportunities to drive returns in our portfolios. Considering environmental, social and governance ESG issues can improve risk-adjusted investment outcomes for our clients and help us build resilient portfolios for the future.

Last fall, IMCO committed to achieving a net zero emissions portfolio by 2050 or earlier and we became a signatory of the Paris Aligned Investment Initiative (PAII).

In November of this year, we announced a milestone on our climate action journey with the disclosure of 2030 interim climate targets.

Consistent with science-based net zero pathways, IMCO will actively reduce its portfolio carbon emissions while concurrently contributing to building the net zero economy. Our interim climate targets are:

  • 50% reduction in portfolio carbon emissions intensity by 2030, as measured against our 2019 baseline; and
  • 20% of the portfolio invested in climate solutions by 2030

The transition to a less carbon intensive economy will create material risks and opportunities for all investors, including our clients. The targets we have set for 2023 reflect our pragmatic approach to helping our clients mitigate the risks and benefit from the opportunities associated with the energy transition.

We are confident in our ability to achieve these targets, especially as we have already made significant investments in the energy transition.

I invite you to read our Climate Action Plan, and our 2021 ESG Report, for more information about our climate strategy and how IMCO will achieve these interim emission reduction targets.

Rossitsa Stoyanova
Chief Investment Officer
